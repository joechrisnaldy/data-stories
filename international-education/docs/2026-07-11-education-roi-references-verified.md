# Verified citations for Post 06 "The Cheapest Degree Abroad Isn't the Best Deal"

Checked 2026-07-11. The cost data is Kaggle; the salary and employment sides are external
(OECD), documented in `external/README.md`.

## Cost dataset

adilshamim8. (2025). Cost of international education [Data set]. Kaggle.
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/adilshamim8/cost-of-international-education
Supports: tuition, rent, visa, insurance, duration per program; 907 programs, 71 countries.

## Average wages (the salary side)

OECD. (2024). Average annual wages [Data set]. OECD Data Explorer.
https://www.oecd.org/en/data/indicators/average-annual-wages.html
Supports: average annual wage per full-time-equivalent dependent employee, 2024, USD PPP,
34 OECD countries. Values in `external/oecd_avg_wages_2024.csv`. Economy-wide, not by field,
not a new-graduate wage.

## Employment rate (the reality-check side)

OECD. (2025). Employment rate [Indicator, ages 15-64]. OECD.
https://www.oecd.org/en/data/indicators/employment-rate.html
Supports: employment rate of the working-age population (15-64), latest available
(2024-2025). Values in `external/employment_rate.csv`. Economy-wide, not new-graduate.

## Framing note

This is an analytical essay, not financial advice. Payback = total degree cost divided by
the destination country's average wage, which assumes the graduate stays and works there;
this assumption is stated plainly and early in the essay.
