# Verified citations for Post 08 "The Score You Didn't Build"

Checked 2026-07-14 against primary sources. Rule applied: verified or omit. All three items

## Dataset

Dey, U. (2024). *Food Nutrition Dataset* [Data set]. Kaggle.
https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/utsavdey1410/food-nutrition-dataset
Supports: five group CSVs, exactly 2,395 foods combined, per-100g nutrient columns, CC0,
published 2024. CONSTRAINTS: it is an unofficial, user-uploaded dataset with no stated
provenance for its values; describe it as a convenience/illustrative dataset, not an
authoritative nutrient database. "Nutrition Density" is the uploader's own derived column, not
a recognized index. Say "2,395" (exact), and "five group CSVs", not "one CSV".

## Atwater energy conversion factors (chart 3, calorie section)

FAO. (2003). *Food energy: Methods of analysis and conversion factors* (FAO Food and Nutrition
Paper 77). Food and Agriculture Organization of the United Nations.
https://www.fao.org/4/y5022e/y5022e00.htm  (Chapter 3: https://www.fao.org/4/y5022e/y5022e04.htm)
Supports: the Atwater general factors, protein 4 kcal/g, carbohydrate 4 kcal/g, fat 9 kcal/g,
alcohol 7 kcal/g (6.9 unrounded). CONSTRAINT: the ~2 kcal/g figure for dietary fibre is a
separate modern metabolizable-energy factor, NOT an "Atwater factor"; in the classic Atwater
system fibre sits inside carbohydrate at 4 kcal/g. Do not call the fibre figure "Atwater's".
Present 4/4/9 (+7 alcohol) as the Atwater general factors; mention fibre's ~2 kcal/g as a
separate add-on if at all. These are rounded general factors; food-specific factors also exist.

## Nutri-Score, published algorithm (constructive foil)

Rayner, M., Scarborough, P., & Lobstein, T. (2009). *The UK Ofcom Nutrient Profiling Model:
Defining 'healthy' and 'unhealthy' foods and drinks for TV advertising to children*. British
Heart Foundation Health Promotion Research Group, University of Oxford.
https://www.ndph.ox.ac.uk/food-ncd/files/about/uk-ofcom-nutrient-profile-model.pdf
Supports: a published per-100g point system, negative components (energy, saturated fat,
sugars, sodium) counter-balanced by positive components. CONSTRAINT: the original model's
positive food component is "fruit, vegetables and nuts". "Legumes" is the Nutri-Score/FSAm-NPS
MODIFICATION (see Deschasaux 2018), not the original UK wording; attribute it correctly.

## Nutri-Score, validated against health outcomes (constructive foil)

Deschasaux, M., Huybrechts, I., Murphy, N., Julia, C., Hercberg, S., Srour, B., Vidal, L.,
Kaaks, R., ... Touvier, M. (2018). Nutritional quality of food as represented by the FSAm-NPS
nutrient profiling system underlying the Nutri-Score label and cancer risk in Europe: Results
from the EPIC prospective cohort study. *PLOS Medicine, 15*(9), e1002651.
https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pmed.1002651
Supports: in the EPIC cohort (471,495 adults, 10 countries), a higher FSAm-NPS score (lower
nutritional quality) was ASSOCIATED with higher overall cancer risk (HR = 1.07; 95% CI
1.03-1.10). CONSTRAINTS: this validates the score against CANCER RISK specifically, in an
OBSERVATIONAL cohort. Say "associated with" (not causal); do NOT claim "mortality" (a companion
paper was not verified here). "Validated against health outcomes" is accurate if framed this way.
