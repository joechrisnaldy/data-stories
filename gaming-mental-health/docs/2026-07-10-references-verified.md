## Prevalence, low end (about 1.90%)

Supports: the low end of the Indonesian adolescent IGD prevalence spread.
Confirmed quote: "This study screened 1.90% of the respondents with risk of IGD using
the original 5-point cutoff." Instrument: Indonesian ten-item Internet Gaming Disorder
Test (IGDT-10). Sample: 1,233 youth video-game players.

APA 7:
Siste, K., Hanafi, E., Sen, L. T., Damayanti, R., Beatrice, E., & Ismail, R. I. (2022).
Psychometric properties of the Indonesian Ten-item Internet Gaming Disorder Test and a
latent class analysis of gamer population among youths. PLOS ONE, 17(6), e0269528.
https://doi.org/10.1371/journal.pone.0269528

## Prevalence, high end (30.8%)

Supports: the high end of the spread.
Confirmed quote: "It was found that 30.8% of the adolescents had already experienced a
gaming disorder." Instrument: Internet Gaming Disorder Scale-Short Form (IGDS9-SF),
Indonesian version. Sample: 616 junior-high students, Banda Aceh.

## WHO gaming disorder (ICD-11)

Supports: gaming disorder is a real, recognized condition (the essay's honesty caveat).
Confirmed: gaming disorder is classified in ICD-11 ("impaired control over gaming,
increasing priority given to gaming... continuation or escalation despite negative
consequences"). Year: ICD-11 adopted by the World Health Assembly in 2019 (in force
2022); citing 2019 for series consistency with Posts 01 to 03.

APA 7:
World Health Organization. (2019). International classification of diseases for mortality
and morbidity statistics (11th ed.). https://icd.who.int/browse11

## Broader-research context (Orben & Przybylski), carried from the methods essay

Supports the essay line: "across hundreds of thousands of real adolescents... a fraction
of a percent of the variation." Confirmed 2026-07-10 (WebSearch of the paper): the
specification-curve analysis spans three datasets totaling n = 355,358 adolescents, and
digital-technology use is associated with well-being at a magnitude that "explains at most
0.4% of the variation in well-being." The essay uses the soft paraphrase, which these
figures support.
